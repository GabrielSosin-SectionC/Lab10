The Pangolin

The pangolin is a distinctive mammal known for its tough, overlapping scales made of keratin, resembling a natural suit of armor. Found in Africa and Asia, pangolins are primarily nocturnal and use their long, sticky tongues to forage for ants and termites, making them unique and vital components of their ecosystems.

Features:

The pangolin is able to curl into a ball in order to hide/protect himself from threats. They are also able to use their long tongues to capture ants and feed themselves. Their hard scales provides them with a form of natural armor against harm.

Some of the benefits the Pangolin has to its environment:

Contributing to the regulation of insect populations
Excavating burrows that help spread nutrients
Protect forests from termite destruction, maintaining a balanced ecosystem.
Aerate the soil through their burrows
Their burrowing process provides shelter and thermal refugia for a range of commensal taxa
